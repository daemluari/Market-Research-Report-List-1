<p><p>Thin film substrates in electronic packaging are integral for miniaturizing components and enhancing performance. Rigid thin-film substrates, typically made from materials like ceramics or glass, provide stable support for high-density interconnections and are ideal for applications requiring durability, such as in automotive or aerospace electronics. In contrast, flexible thin-film substrates are made from materials like polyimide or PET, enabling versatile designs and adaptability in space-constrained environments. These substrates are commonly used in wearable devices and flexible electronics, promoting innovation and functionality.</p></p>
