<p><p>The pulp and paper enzymes market includes various enzyme types that enhance manufacturing processes. Amylases break down starches, improving fiber separation. Xylanases and hemicellulases degrade hemicellulose, boosting pulping efficiency and reducing chemical use. Lipases assist in fat removal during paper processing, ensuring higher quality. Cellulases enhance cellulose degradation, facilitating better fiber utilization. Pectinases help in the removal of pectin, aiding in fiber processing. Together, these enzymes optimize production efficiency, improve product quality, and support sustainability in the pulp and paper industry.</p></p>
