<p><p>Automated CPR devices are categorized primarily into pneumatic-driven and battery-driven types. Pneumatic-driven devices utilize compressed air to deliver consistent chest compressions, providing a reliable and powerful force during cardiac arrest. In contrast, battery-driven devices are powered by rechargeable batteries, making them portable and suitable for various emergency settings. Both types aim to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of CPR, enhancing patient outcomes during critical situations where immediate and high-quality chest compressions are essential.</p></p>
