<p><p>Embolization in interventional oncology is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat tumors by obstructing their blood supply, leading to tumor necrosis. This technique is pivotal in managing liver cancer, kidney tumors, and other malignancies, offering patients an alternative to traditional surgery with fewer complications and shorter recovery times.</p><p>The embolization market in interventional oncology is experiencing substantial growth, fueled by an increasing prevalence of cancer cases worldwide and rising demand for minimally invasive procedures. Technological advancements, such as the development of new embolic materials and imaging techniques, are enhancing the effectiveness and safety of these procedures.
