<p><p>The artificial ligaments market is divided into two primary types: synthetic and biological artificial ligaments. Synthetic artificial ligaments are made from materials like polyethylene or polyester, offering durability and strength for orthopedic applications. They are commonly used in sports medicine and trauma cases. In contrast, biological artificial ligaments are derived from natural tissues, promoting better integration with the body. These ligaments aim to mimic the function and properties of natural ligaments, enhancing healing and reducing rejection rates.</p></p>
