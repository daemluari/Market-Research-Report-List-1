<p><p>The metastatic cancer treatment drug market comprises two main types: chemotherapy drugs and targeted therapy drugs. Chemotherapy drugs work by killing fast-growing cancer cells throughout the body but may also affect healthy cells, leading to side effects. In contrast, targeted therapy drugs are designed to target specific molecular markers associated with cancer cells, minimizing damage to normal cells and offering more precise treatment options. These therapies are critical in managing advanced cancer and improving patient outcomes.</p></p>
