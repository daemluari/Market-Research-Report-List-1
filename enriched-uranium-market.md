<p><p>The enriched uranium market is primarily divided into two types: Low Enriched Uranium (LEU) and Highly Enriched Uranium (HEU). LEU, with uranium-235 content between 3-5%, is mainly used as fuel for nuclear reactors in power generation. In contrast, HEU, containing more than 20% uranium-235, is utilized for military applications and in research reactors. The distinct regulatory and operational requirements for each type significantly influence market dynamics, pricing, and availability, reflecting their varied uses and the geopolitical landscape.</p></p>
