<p><p>The hemodialysis access catheters market consists of two primary types: short-term and long-term catheters. Short-term catheters are typically used for temporary access during acute renal failure or emergency situations, offering quick placement and immediate dialysis capability but usually requiring replacement after several days to weeks. Long-term catheters, on the other hand, are designed for extended use in patients with chronic kidney conditions, remaining functional for months or years, providing a stable access point for regular dialysis sessions.</p></p>
