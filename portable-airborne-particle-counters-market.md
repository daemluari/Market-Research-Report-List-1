<p><p>The Portable Airborne Particle Counters market is segmented into ionizing and non-ionizing types. Ionizing particle counters use radiation to detect and count airborne particles, making them effective in environments with dense aerosol concentrations. Non-ionizing counters, on the other hand, employ light scattering technologies to measure particle concentration and size without using radiation, making them safer for various applications. This division caters to diverse industries, including pharmaceuticals, healthcare, and environmental monitoring, each requiring specific particle detection capabilities.</p></p>
