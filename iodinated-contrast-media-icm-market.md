<p><p>Iodinated Contrast Media (ICM) are substances used in medical imaging to enhance the visibility of internal structures. They are categorized into two primary types: Ionic Contrast Media and Non-Ionic Contrast Media. Ionic Contrast Media contain charged particles, typically resulting in higher osmolarity, which can lead to more side effects and discomfort. In contrast, Non-Ionic Contrast Media have a lower osmolarity and are better tolerated, resulting in fewer adverse reactions. The preference for Non-Ionic forms is driving the market growth.</p></p>
